# Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus Management Study - Protocol Summary

**Trial Name:** LANDMARK-2: Long-term Efficacy and Safety of GLP-1 Agonist Treatment in Type 2 Diabetes

**Phase:** Phase III Randomized Controlled Trial

**Sponsor:** PharmaCorp International

**Target Enrollment:** 1,500 patients

---

## INCLUSION CRITERIA

1. **Diagnosis**: Confirmed diagnosis of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus for at least 6 months prior to screening

2. **Age**: Age 18-75 years at time of consent

3. **Baseline HbA1c**: HbA1c between 7.0% and 10.5% inclusive at screening

4. **BMI**: Body Mass Index (BMI) between 25 and 45 kg/m² inclusive

5. **Kidney Function**: Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR) ≥ 45 mL/min/1.73m² at screening

6. **Cardiovascular Stability**: Stable cardiac status with no hospitalization for acute coronary syndrome or stroke in past 6 months

7. **Stable Diabetes Treatment**: On stable diabetes regimen (metformin and/or sulfonylureas) for at least 8 weeks prior to screening

8. **Informed Consent**: Willing and able to provide written informed consent and comply with study requirements

---

## EXCLUSION CRITERIA

1. **Diabetes Type**: History of Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus or LADA (Latent Autoimmune Diabetes in Adults)

2. **Renal Impairment**: eGFR < 45 mL/min/1.73m² or end-stage renal disease requiring dialysis

3. **Liver Disease**: Active liver disease or liver enzyme abnormalities (ALT or AST > 3x upper limit of normal)

4. **Pancreatitis History**: History of acute or chronic pancreatitis

5. **GLP-1 Prior Use**: Prior or concurrent use of GLP-1 receptor agonists or DPP-4 inhibitors within 12 weeks

6. **Pregnancy Status**: Pregnant, nursing, or planning to become pregnant during study period

7. **Thyroid Malignancy**: Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2

8. **Cardiovascular Event**: Recent myocardial infarction, unstable angina, or stroke within 6 months

9. **Diabetic Complications**: Active proliferative diabetic retinopathy requiring treatment; severe diabetic nephropathy (UACR > 300 mg/g)

10. **Systemic Disease**: Uncontrolled hypertension (SBP > 180 mmHg or DBP > 110 mmHg); severe pulmonary disease or active malignancy

11. **Medication Conflicts**: Use of systemic corticosteroids (except topical) for > 2 weeks in past 3 months; current use of insulin or SGLT2 inhibitors

12. **Other Conditions**: Significant alcohol or drug abuse; conditions that would impair study compliance

---

## STUDY PROCEDURES & EVALUATIONS

**Screening Phase (Week -4 to 0):**
- Medical history, physical examination, vital signs
- Laboratory tests: CBC, chemistry panel, HbA1c, fasting lipids, liver/kidney function
- 12-lead ECG

**Baseline (Week 0):**
- Randomization to treatment or placebo
- Diabetes status and complications assessment

**Treatment Phase (Weeks 0-52):**
- Study visits at weeks 4, 12, 24, 36, 52
- Monthly telephone follow-up calls
- HbA1c measurements at weeks 24 and 52

**Safety Monitoring:**
- Vital signs at each visit
- AE/SAE monitoring throughout study
- Thyroid ultrasound if TSH abnormality detected

---

## STUDY MEDICATION

**Active Arm:** GLP-1 receptor agonist subcutaneous injection, dose-escalated weekly

**Control Arm:** Matching placebo injection

**Concomitant Medications:** Continued stable diabetes regimen; antihypertensives and lipid-lowering agents allowed

---

## KEY ENDPOINTS

**Primary Endpoint:** Change in HbA1c from baseline to Week 52

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Percentage of patients achieving HbA1c < 7%
- Weight change from baseline
- Cardiovascular events and mortality
- Quality of life assessment

**Safety:** Adverse event monitoring, laboratory monitoring, cardiac event tracking

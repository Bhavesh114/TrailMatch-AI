# TrialMatch AI — LinkedIn Post

## Main Post

80% of clinical trials miss their enrollment targets. Not because the patients don't exist. But because finding them takes forever.

Study coordinators spend 15-45 minutes manually screening each patient against trial criteria. They're reading through 20-50 page protocols, cross-referencing lab values, checking medical histories. A single error — missing an exclusion criterion, misreading a lab range — and an ineligible patient gets enrolled, or an eligible one gets rejected.

At a typical academic medical center, coordinators juggle dozens of active trials. The math breaks down fast.

**So I built TrialMatch AI.**

It takes your trial protocol (the PDF), extracts all inclusion/exclusion criteria into a clean, structured format, and then evaluates each criterion against a patient summary in seconds. Not with a yes/no black box. With transparent reasoning: "MEETS this criterion because..." or "INSUFFICIENT DATA — need recent HbA1c value."

The coordinator verifies the first pass in under a minute. AI doesn't decide. It accelerates.

**The key insight:** In healthcare, explainability isn't a nice-to-have. It's the whole point. Screeners need to see the reasoning so they can catch the model's mistakes. That's not a limitation — it's the design.

Built with React, FastAPI, Claude API, and AWS. Currently handling protocols across oncology and cardiology trials.

If you work in clinical research, healthcare AI, or you've felt the friction of manual screening workflows — I'd love to hear your thoughts. Or if you know coordinators running trials at your institution, I'm looking for real-world validation and feedback.

Let's accelerate how we match patients to trials.
